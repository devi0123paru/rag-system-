CARDIAC EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS - EMS FIELD GUIDE

=== PROTOCOL C-1: CHEST PAIN / POSSIBLE CARDIAC EVENT ===
Indications: Patient presents with chest pain, pressure, tightness, or discomfort.

CRITICAL ASSESSMENT SIGNS (treat as STEMI until proven otherwise):
- Chest pain radiating to left arm, jaw, or back
- Diaphoresis (sweating) with chest pain
- Nausea/vomiting with chest discomfort
- Shortness of breath with chest pain
- Pain score > 7/10
- Age > 50 with any chest discomfort

INITIAL ACTIONS:
1. Position patient supine or semi-recumbent (45 degrees if SOB)
2. Administer O2 if SpO2 < 94% (target 94-98%)
3. Obtain 12-lead ECG within 10 minutes of patient contact
4. Establish IV access (large bore, antecubital)
5. Administer Aspirin 324mg PO (chew) if no contraindications
6. Continuous cardiac monitoring
7. Obtain vital signs every 5 minutes

- ST elevation > 1mm in 2 contiguous leads = STEMI â†’ CATH LAB ACTIVATION
- New LBBB with symptoms = STEMI equivalent
- HR < 40 or > 150 with hemodynamic compromise
- SBP < 90 mmHg
- GCS < 14

HOSPITAL ROUTING:
- STEMI confirmed: Route to PCI-capable hospital (door-to-balloon < 90 min)
- Unstable: Nearest emergency department
- Stable, no ST changes: Nearest ED with cardiology

=== PROTOCOL C-2: CARDIAC ARREST ===
IMMEDIATE ACTIONS:
1. Confirm unresponsiveness and absent breathing
2. Call for AED
3. Begin CPR: 30 compressions : 2 breaths ratio
4. Compression depth: 2-2.4 inches adults
5. Rate: 100-120/min
6. Minimize interruptions (< 10 sec)
7. Attach AED/defibrillator ASAP

SHOCKABLE RHYTHMS (VF/pulseless VT):
- Shock at 200J biphasic immediately
- Resume CPR for 2 minutes post-shock
- Epinephrine 1mg IV/IO every 3-5 min
- Amiodarone 300mg IV/IO after 3rd shock

NON-SHOCKABLE (PEA/Asystole):
- CPR continuous
- Epinephrine 1mg IV/IO every 3-5 min
- Treat reversible causes (H's and T's)

=== PROTOCOL C-3: HEART FAILURE / ACUTE PULMONARY EDEMA ===
SIGNS/SYMPTOMS:
- Severe shortness of breath, orthopnea
- Pink frothy sputum
- Bilateral crackles/rales
- JVD present
- SpO2 < 90% on room air

CRITICAL INDICATORS:
- SpO2 < 85% despite O2 = CRITICAL
- RR > 30/min = HIGH
- SBP < 90 = CRITICAL (cardiogenic shock)
- Altered mental status = CRITICAL

TREATMENT:
1. Sit patient upright (legs dependent)
2. High-flow O2, CPAP if available and SpO2 < 90%
3. Nitroglycerin 0.4mg SL (if SBP > 100 and no recent PDE5 inhibitor use)
4. Furosemide 40mg IV if known CHF
5. Monitor continuously

HOSPITAL: Nearest ED; alert for possible ICU admission.
