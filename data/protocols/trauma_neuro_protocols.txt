TRAUMA & NEUROLOGICAL EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

=== PROTOCOL T-1: MAJOR TRAUMA ===
MECHANISM OF INJURY (High-risk MOI → automatic CRITICAL):
- High-speed MVC (> 40 mph), rollover, ejection
- Fall > 20 feet (or > 3x patient height)
- Penetrating injury to head/neck/torso
- Motorcycle crash > 20 mph
- Pedestrian/cyclist struck by vehicle

PRIMARY SURVEY (ABCDE):
A - Airway: Clear; jaw thrust if C-spine concern; intubate if GCS ≤ 8
B - Breathing: RR, SpO2, bilateral breath sounds, tension pneumo?
C - Circulation: HR, BP, capillary refill, control hemorrhage (direct pressure, tourniquet)
D - Disability: GCS, pupils, BGL
E - Exposure: Full body exam, maintain warmth

CRITICALITY ASSESSMENT:
CRITICAL (any one present):
- GCS < 14
- SBP < 90 mmHg despite fluid
- RR < 10 or > 29
- SpO2 < 90%
- Penetrating head/chest/abdomen injury
- Suspected pelvic fracture with instability
- Amputation proximal to wrist/ankle

HIGH:
- GCS 14 with mechanism
- Two or more long bone fractures
- SBP 90-100 with tachycardia

GOLDEN HOUR PRINCIPLE: Transport time to definitive care should not exceed 60 minutes total.
PREFERRED HOSPITAL: Level I Trauma Center. If > 30 min away and unstable, go to nearest surgical facility.

=== PROTOCOL N-1: STROKE (FAST Protocol) ===
RECOGNITION (BE-FAST):
B - Balance: Sudden loss of balance or coordination
E - Eyes: Sudden vision changes
F - Face drooping (ask to smile)
A - Arm weakness (raise both arms)
S - Speech difficulty (repeat a sentence)
T - Time to call 911 / Time of symptom onset

CRITICAL TIME WINDOWS:
- tPA eligible: Onset < 4.5 hours → STROKE CENTER REQUIRED
- Thrombectomy eligible: Onset < 24 hours with imaging → Comprehensive Stroke Center
- Wake-up stroke: Use last known well time

PREHOSPITAL STROKE SCALE (Cincinnati):
- Facial droop: Normal (symmetric) vs Abnormal (one side droops)
- Arm drift: Normal (both stay) vs Abnormal (one drifts/falls)
- Speech: Normal (correct words, clear) vs Abnormal (slurred/wrong words/mute)
Any ONE abnormal = 72% probability of stroke

CRITICALITY:
CRITICAL: Any positive BEFAST finding → Stroke Alert activation
- Large vessel occlusion signs: gaze deviation + hemiplegia + aphasia
- Hemorrhagic signs: sudden severe headache "thunderclap", vomiting, coma

HOSPITAL ROUTING:
- Suspected stroke: ALWAYS bypass non-stroke hospitals
- Thrombectomy capable center preferred if < 30 min extra transport time
- Pre-notify: "Stroke Alert, ETA [X] minutes, NIHSS estimate [score]"

PREHOSPITAL CARE:
1. Do NOT give glucose unless BGL confirmed < 60
2. O2 only if SpO2 < 94%
3. Keep HOB at 0-15 degrees (flat) unless vomiting
4. NPO — aspiration risk
5. Obtain IV access, 12-lead
6. Do NOT delay transport for extended on-scene care

=== PROTOCOL N-2: SEIZURE ===
TYPES AND MANAGEMENT:
Generalized Tonic-Clonic: Protect from injury, do not restrain, time seizure
Status Epilepticus (> 5 min or no recovery): CRITICAL → Benzodiazepine protocol

CRITICALITY:
CRITICAL:
- Active seizure > 5 min
- First-time seizure with altered LOC post-ictally
- Seizure with fever > 39°C (meningitis risk)
- Post-traumatic seizure
- Pregnancy + seizure (eclampsia)
- No return to baseline after 30 min

TREATMENT STATUS EPILEPTICUS:
1. Midazolam 10mg IM (or 5mg IV/IO) → First-line
2. If no response in 5 min: Lorazepam 4mg IV
3. If no response: Levetiracetam 60mg/kg IV (max 4.5g)
4. Continuous: Call medical direction; consider RSI

HOSPITAL: Nearest ED; Level II+ for status epilepticus.
