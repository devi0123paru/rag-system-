TRIAGE PROTOCOLS & PATIENT CRITICALITY ASSESSMENT

=== PREHOSPITAL TRIAGE DECISION GUIDE ===

CRITICALITY LEVELS:
■ CRITICAL (Red) — Immediate life threat, minutes matter
■ HIGH (Orange) — Serious, potential deterioration, < 30 min
■ MODERATE (Yellow) — Urgent but stable, < 60 min
■ LOW (Green) — Non-urgent, can wait
■ BLACK — Deceased or non-survivable given resources

=== UNIVERSAL CRITICAL VITAL SIGN THRESHOLDS ===
Any ONE of these = CRITICAL classification:
- SpO2 < 90% on oxygen
- RR < 8 or > 36 breaths/min
- Heart Rate < 40 or > 180 bpm
- Systolic BP < 80 mmHg (adult)
- GCS ≤ 8
- Temperature > 40°C or < 32°C
- BGL < 40 mg/dL or > 600 mg/dL

=== SHOCK INDEX (SI) ===
Formula: SI = Heart Rate / Systolic BP
- SI < 0.6: Normal
- SI 0.6–0.9: Normal (borderline)
- SI 1.0–1.19: Moderate shock (CLASS II hemorrhage)
- SI ≥ 1.2: Severe shock — CRITICAL — aggressive resuscitation

=== RESPIRATORY EMERGENCIES ===

PROTOCOL R-1: ANAPHYLAXIS
CRITICAL indicators:
- Airway: stridor, uvula swelling, hoarse voice
- Respiratory: wheezing, SpO2 < 93%
- Cardiovascular: SBP < 90, syncope
- Cutaneous alone: LOW criticality (may worsen)

TREATMENT:
1. Epinephrine 0.3mg IM (lateral thigh) — IMMEDIATELY
2. O2 high-flow
3. IV access; fluid bolus 1-2L if hypotensive
4. Diphenhydramine 50mg IV/IM
5. Methylprednisolone 125mg IV
6. Repeat epi in 5-15 min if no improvement
7. Glucagon 1-2mg IV if on beta-blockers

PROTOCOL R-2: RESPIRATORY DISTRESS
CRITICAL:
- SpO2 < 88% despite BVM
- Agonal respirations
- Apnea
- Epiglottitis (do not examine oropharynx, keep calm, go to OR-capable hospital)

HIGH:
- SpO2 88-93% with increased work of breathing
- RR > 30
- Accessory muscle use
- Unable to complete sentences

=== OBSTETRIC EMERGENCIES ===

PROTOCOL O-1: IMMINENT DELIVERY
Assess crowning — if visible: prepare for field delivery
CRITICAL indicators:
- Cord prolapse (CRITICAL — elevate presenting part, immediate transport)
- Breech presentation (CRITICAL)
- Shoulder dystocia (McRoberts maneuver, suprapubic pressure)
- Heavy bleeding with pregnancy (placenta previa/abruption)
- Pre-eclampsia: BP > 160/110 + headache/visual changes

=== PEDIATRIC CONSIDERATIONS ===

PEDIATRIC VITAL SIGN NORMS (approximate):
Age        HR        RR      SBP
Newborn   100-160  40-60   60-80
1 year    90-150   24-40   80-90
5 years   70-120   18-30   90-100
10 years  60-100   16-24   100-115

Pediatric Shock: SBP < 70 + (2 × age in years) = CRITICAL
Fever > 38°C in neonate (< 3 months) = CRITICAL

=== PAIN SCALE & CRITICALITY ===
Pain 0-3: LOW (unless with critical vitals)
Pain 4-6: MODERATE
Pain 7-9: HIGH
Pain 10: HIGH to CRITICAL depending on presentation

=== MULTI-CASUALTY INCIDENT (MCI) TRIAGE ===
START Triage (Simple Triage And Rapid Treatment):

Step 1: Walking wounded? → GREEN (minor)
Step 2: Breathing? No → Open airway → Still no → BLACK
Step 3: RR > 30 → RED (critical)
Step 4: Radial pulse absent / cap refill > 2s → RED
Step 5: Follow commands? No → RED
Step 6: All others → YELLOW (delayed)

MCI Dispatch Rule: Do not transport all patients to nearest hospital. Distribute across facilities to avoid ED overwhelm.
